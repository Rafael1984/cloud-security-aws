Roleplay — Simulate real Cloud Security conversations
01
Read today's scenario and understand the context
Each scenario simulates a real situation: an IR call, architecture review, CISO meeting, or interview. Read the title, difficulty badge, and context before starting.
02
Copy the prompt and open ChatGPT or Grok in voice mode
Click 📋 Copy Prompt. Open your AI of choice and switch to voice mode (not text). Paste the prompt. The AI will play a character — you respond in real-time in English.
03
Speak for at least 10–20 minutes
Use the support phrases listed on the card (click to copy). Use the vocabulary chips — mark them when you use each one. The goal is to speak continuously without long pauses.
04
Check the Connected Speech boxes
You must mark at least 2 Connected Speech patterns (gonna, wanna, lemme, gotta) before you can mark the session as complete. This ensures you're speaking naturally, not formally.
05
After the roleplay — request corrections from the AI
Ask: "Now list all the mistakes I made using the ORIGINAL / CORRECTION / TYPE format." Copy the response. Go to ⛏️ Mistakes to generate your Anki cards.
🎭The AI is your scene partner. If you freeze, say: "Let me rephrase that" or "Give me a moment to think." These are real professional phrases, not signs of weakness.
🔁Repeat the same scenario on different days until it feels automatic. Real fluency comes from repetition, not variety.
📌The phrase library below the card contains 112 real professional phrases organized by context. Browse it after each roleplay.

▼ HOW TO USE THIS MODULE STEP 6 · ANKI Mistake Mining — Turn every mistake into a flashcard
Mistake Mining — Turn every mistake into a flashcard
01
Copy the AI correction prompt from the box above
Click 📋 Copy Prompt. This prompt is specifically designed to extract mistakes in the correct format. Send it to the AI after your roleplay as a follow-up message.
02
Paste the AI's correction list into the text area
The AI will return a list with ORIGINAL / CORRECTION / TYPE / EXPLANATION for each mistake. Copy the entire response and paste it in the Paste Corrections box on the left.
03
Click Generate Cards From Mistakes
The system automatically parses your mistakes and generates up to 7 flashcard types per error: grammar correction, natural English, fill-in-the-blank, translation, collocation, interview format, and Cloud Security term.
04
Review and export your cards to Anki
Your generated cards appear on the right. Choose your export format (TSV is the default for Anki). Click 📋 Copy for Anki. Open Anki → File → Import → paste. Done.
05
Check the Mistake Scoreboard
Mistakes that appear 3 or more times are automatically elevated to High Priority. These are your most persistent patterns — the ones that will actually hold you back in real conversations.
⚡Do this immediately after every roleplay — while the context is fresh. Don't save it for later. The whole process takes 3–5 minutes.
🎯The most valuable cards are Collocation and Cloud Security Term types — these are the mistakes that make you sound non-native in a professional context.
📊The Scoreboard shows your top 10 recurring mistakes. If the same mistake appears 5+ times, make it a Shadowing phrase — say it out loud until it's automatic.

▼ HOW TO USE THIS MODULE STEP 2 Listening — Train your ear for technical English
Listening — Train your ear for technical English
01
Pick a source from the playlist below
Choose a podcast, conference talk, or recording that matches your level. SANS Stormcast is 5 minutes — perfect for a quick session. Darknet Diaries builds narrative fluency for IR calls.
02
Listen without pausing — full immersion
Do not pause to look up words. Listen from start to finish. Your brain needs to process real spoken speed. The first 10 times you hear a podcast you'll miss things — that's normal and expected.
03
Log your session immediately after
Select the type, enter minutes, rate your comprehension honestly, and click ✓ Log. Your stats update automatically and count toward your daily checklist.
04
Build your listening streak
Even 5 minutes daily is enough. The listening streak counter at the top tracks consecutive days. A 30-day streak will transform your comprehension speed.
🔁Re-listen to the same episode 2–3 times over a week. You'll catch 30% more each time. This is called "repeated listening" and it's one of the fastest ways to improve.
✍️After listening, try to summarize what you heard in English — even just 2 sentences. This forces active processing.
🎯Goal: 50% of your total study time should be listening. It builds vocabulary, rhythm, and comprehension simultaneously.

▼ HOW TO USE THIS MODULE STEP 3 Shadowing — Speak exactly like a native professional
Shadowing — Speak exactly like a native professional
01
Pick any phrase from the list below
Each card contains a real sentence used by Cloud Security professionals in interviews, IR calls, or architecture reviews. Start with phrases from the IR Call or Architecture category.
02
Read the phrase out loud — then say it again faster
Read it naturally first. Then try to say it at native speed — connected, without pausing between words. Focus on rhythm and flow, not just pronunciation.
03
Record yourself with the microphone button
Hit 🎙️ Record, say the phrase, then hit ⏹️ Stop. The audio player appears instantly. Listen to your own recording and compare it to how a native would say it.
04
Mark a repetition and aim for 5x per phrase
Click ▶ Repeat after each attempt. The 5 dots track your repetitions. A phrase is mastered after 5 reps. Come back to it tomorrow — spaced repetition matters.
🎯Don't aim for a perfect accent. Aim for clear, natural rhythm. Most Cloud Security professionals speak with various accents — clarity and fluency matter more than sound.
⚡The most important phrases are IR Call ones: "I'm gonna revoke the credentials", "Lemme pull up the logs", "The blast radius could be significant." These appear in real calls.
📱You can do shadowing anywhere — on the bus, in the car. You don't need to record every time. Just saying the phrase 5x out loud is enough.

▼ HOW TO USE THIS MODULE STEP 5 Collocations — Think and speak in complete language blocks
Collocations — Think and speak in complete language blocks
01
Browse the categories using the tabs
Collocations are organized into 4 categories: Incident Response, Architecture & Design, Cloud & IAM, and Meetings & Governance. Each contains 5 high-frequency professional phrases.
02
Read the definition and corporate example
For each collocation, read the definition (what it means), the example sentence (how it's used in a real meeting), and the mistake vs natural comparison — this shows what Brazilians typically say wrong.
03
Practice it out loud in a sentence
Before marking it as mastered, say the full example sentence out loud. Then try to create your own sentence with the same collocation. This is what builds real fluency.
04
Mark as mastered when it feels natural
Click Mark mastered when you can use the phrase without thinking about it. Mastered collocations update your Dashboard progress bar.
05
Generate an Anki card for anything you want to review
Click 🃏 Anki on any collocation to open the card generator pre-filled with the phrase, definition, and example. Export it directly to your Anki deck.
🔗"Conduct a security review" is one block — you don't say "do a security review" or "make a security review." Native speakers hear the wrong collocation immediately.
⭐Priority: learn Incident Response collocations first. These appear in every IR call: "contain the breach", "revoke credentials", "preserve forensic evidence".
🔁Once per week, go through all collocations quickly and say each one in a sentence. This prevents forgetting.
